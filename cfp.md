# Call for Papers

We invite submissions on topics at the intersection of **model-based reinforcement learning**, **generative world models**, and **embodied AI**. To encourage work-in-progress and future conference submissions, we accept **non-archival contributions** through a double-blind review process via [OpenReview](https://openreview.net/).

## Topics of Interest

- Scalable transition dynamics using generative architectures (video diffusion, autoregressive models, etc.)
- Compounding error mitigation in learned world models
- Policy extraction and planning within learned simulators
- Zero-shot and few-shot generalization from world model pre-training
- Offline RL and synthetic data generation for embodied pre-training
- Action-aware and control-relevant representations for MBRL
- Sim-to-real transfer using generative world models
- Theoretical analysis of MBRL with learned dynamics
- Evaluation benchmarks for MBRL in high-dimensional and noisy environments
- Embodied AI applications: robotics, autonomous driving, navigation

## Submission Guidelines

- **Format:** Non-archival; accepted papers will not appear in formal proceedings.
- **Review:** Double-blind via OpenReview. Organizers recuse themselves from reviewing submissions with conflicts of interest, including institutional affiliations.
- **Length:** Up to 4 pages (excluding references). Appendices allowed but may not be reviewed.
- **Format:** Please use the [RLC 2026 style files](https://rl-conference.cc/).
